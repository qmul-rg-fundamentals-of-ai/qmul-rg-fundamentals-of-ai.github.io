Welcome to the QMUL reading group, where we explore the fundamental principles of AI. Our past sessions have revolved around the following papers (not an exhaustive list):

- Understanding Generalization through Visualizations by W. Ronny Huang, et al. Available at: [https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.03291](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.03291)

- Deep Ensembles a Loss Landscape Perspective by Stanislav Fort, Huiyi Hu, and Balaji Lakshminarayanan.  Available at: [https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.02757](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.02757) 

- What Makes a Good Prune? Maximal Unstructured Pruning for Maximal Cosine Similarity by Gabryel Mason-Williams and Fredrick Dahlqvist. Available at: [https://openreview.net/forum?id=jsvvPVVzwf](https://openreview.net/forum?id=jsvvPVVzwf) 

- Predicting trends in the quality of state-of-the-art neural networks without access to training or testing data by Charles H. Martin, Tongsu (Serena) Peng & Michael W. Mahoney. Available at: [https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-24025-8](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-24025-8)

- Measuring the Intrinsic Dimension of Objective Landscapes by Chunyuan Li, Heerad Farkhoor, Rosanne Liu and Jason Yosinski. Available at: [https://openreview.net/pdf?id=ryup8-WCW](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=ryup8-WCW) 

If you would like to present a paper or talk about current research, ideas/experiments you would like to collaborate on or explore that are related to the Fundamentals of AI, however broad or detailed, please message <gabryel.t.mason-williams@qmul.ac.uk> to present or attend one of the meetings this semester.
